For most chiropractors, the single most valuable piece of real estate online isn't their website — it's the little map that shows up when someone searches "chiropractor near me." Three practices get featured. Everyone else scrolls.

Getting into that map pack isn't luck, and it isn't about who's been around longest. It comes down to a handful of things Google is actually measuring.

## 1. Your Google Business Profile is the product

Most practices set up their profile once and forget it. That's a mistake — it's the single biggest lever you have. Fill out *everything*: the right primary category, every relevant service, accurate hours, photos, and a real description. An incomplete profile tells Google you're not the most relevant result.

## 2. Reviews — recent, steady, and answered

Reviews influence local rankings more than almost anything else, but it's not just the star rating. Google looks at how *many* you have, how *recent* they are, and increasingly what they *say*. A steady trickle of fresh reviews beats a big pile from three years ago. And reply to them — all of them.

## 3. Consistency everywhere (NAP)

Your **N**ame, **A**ddress, and **P**hone number need to match exactly across your website, your profile, and every directory you appear in. Mismatched info makes Google less confident it's the same business — and confidence is what gets you ranked.

## 4. Proximity is real (so own your relevance)

Part of local ranking is simple distance from the searcher, which you can't control. What you *can* control is being so clearly relevant and trusted that you win whenever you're in range — through everything above, plus a website that reinforces the same location and services.

## The compounding part

None of these is a one-time task. The practice that updates its profile, earns reviews every week, and keeps its information consistent will quietly climb and *stay* there — while competitors who "set it and forgot it" wonder why they slipped. Local visibility is a system, not a setting.
