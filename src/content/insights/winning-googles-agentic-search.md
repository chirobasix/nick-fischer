For twenty years, "getting found on Google" meant one thing: rank a page high enough that someone clicks it. That era is ending. Patients are increasingly getting their answer *before* they ever click — from AI overviews, from ChatGPT, from a voice assistant that just tells them who to call.

This is the shift behind what I call agentic search: an AI doesn't hand you ten blue links, it makes a recommendation. And for a chiropractor, the only question that matters is whether you're the practice it recommends.

## You're no longer optimizing for a ranking. You're optimizing to be the answer.

Old SEO optimized a page for a keyword. The new game is **answer engine optimization** — making it effortless for an AI to understand who you are, what you do, where you are, and why you're trustworthy, and then to repeat that confidently to a patient.

That changes what you invest in.

## What actually moves the needle

- **Be unambiguous about your entity.** An AI needs to know "this practice = this name, this location, these services, these hours" with zero contradiction. Inconsistent names, addresses, or phone numbers across the web are the fastest way to get left out.
- **Structured data and a clean Google profile** give the machine something concrete to read instead of guessing.
- **Reviews are now training data.** When an AI weighs which practice to recommend, the volume and substance of your reviews is one of the clearest trust signals it has.
- **Answer the real questions.** The content that wins isn't keyword-stuffed — it directly answers what patients ask: "Is this safe?" "Do you take my insurance?" "What should I expect on the first visit?"

## The practices that win will look boring

There's no trick here. The chiropractors who win agentic search will be the ones who are the *clearest, most consistent, and most trusted* entity in their market — online and off. That's not a campaign you run. It's a foundation you build once and keep clean.

The AI is just going to recommend whoever is easiest to recommend. Make that you.
